MURKOFF CORP

COPY

TRANSCRIPTION FROM TAPE OF CONFERENCE BETWEEN A. BRADLEY AVELLANOS, H.J. EASTERMAN, and MOSES SCARFIOTTI

EDITED FOR CLARITY

(minutes 22-26)

AVELLANOS: We'll still need a good pitch for The Board. The Agency's been complaining about a collateral damage in operations.

EASTERMAN: Levers of control. I don't think I need to repeat myself.

SCARFIOTTI: Is this not what you said about Invasion?

EASTERMAN: What do you think I said?

SCARFIOTTI: That it would give you greater control over Reagents.

EASTERMAN: And?

AVELLANOS: And it's almost comedic. Reagents only became more aggressive. They're bullying the Imposters.

EASTERMAN: We're making killers.

SCARFIOTTI: Invasion will need material revisions.  I have a proposal for re-balancing tools.

EASTERMAN: It's addressable on a psychological level. Reagents will identify with the Warden. They'll see that Coyle is a bully, and that Coyle is subject to my authority. To Murkoff's.

AVELLANOS: What do you expect Coyle to do with the Warden?

EASTERMAN: He'll just push him around a little, I'm sure. Call him some names.

SCARFIOTTI: Do you really expect to see a difference in the social engineering of a prison environment.

EASTERMAN: A prison is a lens for focusing authority. The prisoners cannot help but understand who is in charge.

SCARFIOTTI: It's redundant, a prison inside a prison.

EASTERMAN: I resent the implication. Sinyala is not a prison. It is the polar opposite of the prison. I sometimes wonder if you are intentionally misunderstanding the work we do here.
